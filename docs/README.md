# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

## Overview

OctoAcme follows a structured, iterative approach to project management that prioritizes customer value, clear ownership, and data-informed decisions. Our framework spans five key phases—Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Retrospective—ensuring alignment and accountability at every stage.

**Core Principles:**
- **Customer-first**: Prioritize customer value and usability in all decisions
- **Iterative delivery**: Deliver small, testable increments rather than monolithic releases
- **Clear ownership**: Each project has named Project Manager and Product Lead roles
- **Data-informed decisions**: Measure impact and iterate based on evidence
- **Psychological safety**: Encourage feedback, learning, and continuous improvement

The OctoAcme process emphasizes lightweight upfront validation, structured planning, quality-embedded execution, and continuous learning through retrospectives. Key to our approach is maintaining a single source of truth through project documentation, clear communication cadences across team roles, and defined escalation paths for risks and blockers.

## Key Artifacts Used Across OctoAcme

- **Project Charter / One-pager** – Problem statement, goals, success metrics, timeline, risks, and resource needs
- **Risk Register** – Tracked risks with impact, probability, owner, and mitigation plans
- **Project Board** – GitHub Projects with standardized columns: Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, Done
- **Sprint/Iteration Backlog** – Prioritized work items with acceptance criteria and estimates
- **Definition of Done** – Checklist for what "done" means (tests, reviews, documentation, etc.)
- **Release Plan** – Milestones, dependencies, and deployment timeline
- **Retrospective Notes** – Learnings and action items from completed phases or sprints

## Communication Cadence

- **Daily**: Team standups (15 min) – progress, blockers, dependencies
- **Twice-weekly**: Delivery team standups or as agreed
- **Weekly**: PM + PdM sync for planning and coordination
- **Weekly**: Risk register review in delivery syncs
- **Monthly**: Stakeholder updates and status reports
- **Ad-hoc**: Escalations and incident communications as needed
