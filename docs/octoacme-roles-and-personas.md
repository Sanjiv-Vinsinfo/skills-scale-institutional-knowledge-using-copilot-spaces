# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Core Project Delivery Roles

### Developers

#### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

#### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Collaborate with QA and architects on design and implementation

#### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

#### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed
- Collaboration with QA on test coverage

---

### Product Managers

#### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

#### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics
- Accept completed work and validate against acceptance criteria
- Partner with Business Analysts on requirements

#### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

#### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs
- User feedback and validation results

---

### Project Managers

#### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

#### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication
- Partner with Scrum Masters on process and impediment removal
- Track resource allocation and team capacity

#### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

#### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation
- Escalation and dependency management

---

## Specialist and Supporting Roles

### QA/Testing Lead

#### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads own the quality strategy and ensure that features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release. They collaborate with product and development teams to plan testing approaches and validate solutions.

#### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and approach for each project phase
- Create and maintain test plans and test cases
- Execute manual testing and validate acceptance criteria
- Identify and log defects with clear reproduction steps
- Participate in acceptance testing with Product Managers
- Recommend improvements to testing processes and tooling
- Mentor developers on testing best practices
- Collaborate with architects on testability and test infrastructure

#### Goals
- Ensure high-quality, defect-free releases
- Reduce production incidents through thorough validation
- Enable fast, confident deployment

#### Typical Communication
- Test status updates in standups and weekly syncs
- Defect reports and quality metrics
- Collaboration with developers on test coverage
- Test plan reviews and quality assessments

#### Interaction with Core Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test coverage, test automation, and defect resolution
- **Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria and conduct user acceptance testing
- **Project Managers**: Provide quality metrics and readiness assessments for release

---

### Technical Architect

#### Role Summary
Technical Architects define the technical direction, guide system design decisions, and ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with technical standards. They provide oversight on technology choices and design patterns.

#### Responsibilities
- Define system architecture and technical direction
- Review technical design proposals and provide guidance
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Ensure solutions follow coding standards and best practices
- Guide technology choices and platform decisions
- Mentor developers on architectural principles
- Support capacity planning and scalability assessments
- Collaborate with DevOps on operational readiness

#### Goals
- Ensure systems are scalable, maintainable, and resilient
- Reduce technical debt and improve system health
- Enable team velocity through clear architectural guidance

#### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture decisions
- Guidance on technology selections
- Risk assessments and mitigation recommendations
- Architecture documentation and standards

#### Interaction with Core Roles
- **Developers**: Provide design guidance and code review on architectural patterns
- **Project Managers**: Escalate technical risks and estimate architectural effort
- **DevOps Engineers**: Collaborate on infrastructure and scalability requirements

---

### Scrum Master / Facilitator

#### Role Summary
Scrum Masters enable team ceremonies, remove process blockers, and coach the team on agile practices. They protect the team's focus and facilitate effective collaboration.

#### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove impediments and blockers preventing progress
- Coach team on agile principles and practices
- Track sprint metrics and velocity
- Facilitate communication between team and stakeholders
- Suggest process improvements based on team feedback
- Support team in maintaining sustainable pace
- Collaborate with Project Manager on schedule and dependencies

#### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Build a high-performing, self-organizing team
- Reduce meeting overhead while improving alignment

#### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of ceremonies and meetings
- Impediment logs and resolution tracking
- Agile metrics and process feedback
- Team health and retrospective insights

#### Interaction with Core Roles
- **Project Managers**: Partner on impediment removal and process improvement
- **Developers**: Coach on agile practices and facilitate team collaboration
- **All roles**: Ensure effective communication and psychological safety

---

### UX/Design Lead

#### Role Summary
UX/Design Leads own the user experience and ensure features are usable, accessible, and aligned with design standards. They collaborate with product and development teams to deliver user-centered solutions.

#### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create design specs, wireframes, and prototypes
- Establish design system standards and guidelines
- Review designs for usability and accessibility
- Collaborate with developers on implementation fidelity
- Iterate on designs based on user feedback
- Mentor team on UX best practices
- Partner with Product Managers on user experience strategy

#### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, delightful user experiences
- Ensure consistency across products and features
- Reduce user friction and improve adoption

#### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions
- Usability testing results and recommendations
- Design system updates and guidelines
- Accessibility and design standards documentation

#### Interaction with Core Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on user research and feature prioritization
- **Developers**: Provide design specs and iterate on implementation
- **QA/Testing**: Validate accessibility and usability compliance

---

### DevOps/Infrastructure Engineer

#### Role Summary
DevOps/Infrastructure Engineers manage deployment pipelines, infrastructure, and operational readiness. They enable fast, reliable deployments and maintain system reliability.

#### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain deployment pipelines and CI/CD
- Manage infrastructure, scaling, and availability
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting
- Support incident response and troubleshooting
- Optimize performance and resource utilization
- Document operational procedures and runbooks
- Collaborate with development on operability
- Partner with architects on infrastructure requirements

#### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable deployments
- Maintain high availability and performance
- Reduce operational overhead and incidents

#### Typical Communication
- Deployment status and readiness updates
- Infrastructure and performance metrics
- Incident response and resolution
- Operational runbooks and procedures

#### Interaction with Core Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on deployment practices and observability
- **Project Managers**: Provide readiness assessments and deployment timelines
- **Technical Architects**: Ensure infrastructure aligns with architecture

---

### Business Analyst

#### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and delivery teams. They translate business requirements into actionable specifications and validate that solutions meet business needs.

#### Responsibilities
- Gather and clarify business requirements
- Translate requirements into acceptance criteria
- Validate solutions meet business objectives
- Identify process improvements and optimization opportunities
- Support stakeholder communication and training
- Document business processes and workflows
- Assist with impact analysis and change management
- Partner with Product Managers on requirements translation

#### Goals
- Ensure solutions deliver business value
- Reduce rework due to requirement misalignment
- Enable smooth adoption of new features

#### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and clarification
- Business process workflows and specs
- Stakeholder feedback and validation
- Change impact analysis and communication

#### Interaction with Core Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Project Managers**: Provide impact analysis and change management support
- **Stakeholders**: Translate business needs and validate solutions

---

Stakeholders and Sponsors provide strategic direction, funding decisions, and executive oversight. They represent business interests and ensure alignment with organizational objectives.

#### Responsibilities
- Define strategic objectives and business goals
- Make funding and priority decisions
- Provide executive sponsorship and air cover
- Communicate project status to leadership
- Escalate strategic risks and blockers
- Approve scope and timeline changes
- Ensure alignment with organizational strategy
- Participate in key project milestones and reviews

#### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value
- Maximize return on investment
- Maintain strategic alignment across portfolio

#### Typical Communication
- Monthly status updates and health reports
- Strategic guidance and priority setting
- Executive decisions on scope and resources
- Milestone and release announcements

## How These Personas are Used in OctoAcme Projects

### Role Combinations

Not every project requires all roles. Teams size based on project scope:

- **Small projects**: Core roles (PM, PdM, Developers) + QA
- **Medium projects**: Core roles + QA + Technical Architect + Business Analyst
- **Large programs**: Full set of roles with clear ownership and escalation paths

### Cross-Functional Collaboration

Successful OctoAcme projects depend on clear collaboration between roles:

1. **Planning phase**: PdM + PM + Technical Architect + Business Analyst define scope
2. **Design phase**: Technical Architect + UX/Design Lead + Developers collaborate on approach
3. **Execution phase**: Developers + QA + DevOps work together on delivery
4. **Release phase**: All roles contribute to deployment readiness and communication
5. **Retrospective**: All roles reflect on learnings and improvements

### Communication Patterns

- **Daily**: Standups include all active roles
- **Weekly**: Role-specific syncs (PM+PdM, Technical leads, QA)
- **Milestone**: All-hands review and retrospectives
- **Escalations**: Clear paths through role hierarchy (Team → PM → PdM → Sponsor)
